# Community Covenant

HookBus is developed by Agentic Thinking Limited. We are a company, not a charity, and we reserve the right to build a commercial business around this software. We also know that trust is the operating system of open source, and trust is earned by sticking to what you say.

This covenant lists commitments we make to our contributors and users. These are cultural, not legal, but we treat them as binding on ourselves.

## What we will always do

1. **Accept contributions without a signature.** Opening a pull request is all the acceptance we need for the Contributor Licence Agreement. We will never require a contributor to sign a PDF, fax a form, or provide a physical address.

2. **Keep Apache 2.0 releases Apache 2.0 forever.** If we ever move a project to a non-Apache licence, every release previously published under Apache 2.0 remains permanently available under that licence. Community forks of those releases are free to continue.

3. **Convert back to Apache 2.0 within 4 years.** If we move a project to the Business Source License or a similar time-limited licence, each affected release converts back to Apache 2.0 no later than the fourth anniversary of its publication.

4. **Announce licence changes 90 days in advance.** If we ever change a project's licence, we will publish the change, the reasons, and the effective date at least 90 days before it takes effect, in a public issue on the affected repository.

5. **Accept community-authored backports.** If we move a project to a non-Apache licence, we will continue to accept security and correctness patches from the community back into the Apache 2.0 line for as long as that line remains supported.

## What we will never do

1. **Never introduce a Corporate Contributor Licence Agreement (CCLA).** Contributors sign the individual CLA by submitting, and that is final. No separate corporate paperwork.

2. **Never retroactively relicense a release.** Releases published under a given licence keep that licence forever.

3. **Never require copyright assignment.** You keep ownership of your Contributions. You grant us a broad licence to use them; you do not transfer ownership.

4. **Never restrict community use of our protocol or envelope specification.** The HookBus envelope format and wire protocol are, and will always be, freely implementable by anyone under any licence, including commercial products that compete with us.

## Why this matters

HashiCorp, MongoDB, Elastic, and Sentry all changed licences at some point. Community trust is how each of them survived the change. We are writing down, up front, what we will and will not do so that if we ever face the same decision, we are bound by what we promised today, not whatever is convenient tomorrow.
